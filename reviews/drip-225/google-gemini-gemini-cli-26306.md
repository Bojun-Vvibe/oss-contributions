## Notes
- Caps the previously-unbounded "fall back to a different model and reset the attempt counter" retry loop by introducing `DEFAULT_MAX_FALLBACK_COUNT = 3` at `retry.ts:21` and a `resetCount` accumulator at `:259` that increments at every successful fallback (`:319`/`:374`) and is checked at three call sites: persistent-429-fallback at `:313-318`, validation-required at `:342-347`, and max-attempts-fallback at `:378-385`. Without this fix, a backend stuck returning 500 caused `attempt = 0` to be reset on every fallback, never reaching `maxAttempts`, producing an infinite loop visible to users as "the CLI hung."
- The check-then-increment ordering at `:313-319` is correct (`if (resetCount >= DEFAULT_RETRY_OPTIONS.maxFallbackCount) throw` *before* the `await onPersistent429`, then `resetCount++` only inside the `if (fallbackModel)` arm) ‚Äî this means failed fallback attempts (where `onPersistent429` returns null/throws) don't burn the counter, so the user gets the full 3 *successful* model swaps before abort. Defensive in the right direction.
- The new test `should not loop infinitely if the fallback model also returns 500` at `retry.test.ts:742-763` pins the contract by exact value: `expect(fallbackCallback).toHaveBeenCalledTimes(DEFAULT_MAX_FALLBACK_COUNT)` proves the cap is hit at exactly 3 calls ‚Äî a future regression that bumps the constant or removes the guard would break this test immediately. Plus the `expect(promise).rejects.toThrow('Persistent Backend Error')` at `:758` confirms the *original* classified error propagates rather than a synthetic "max fallback exceeded" error, preserving error-message contract for downstream `instanceof RetryableQuotaError` checks.
- Adding `maxFallbackCount` to `RetryOptions` at `:31` and `DEFAULT_RETRY_OPTIONS` at `:43` keeps the surface configurable for callers needing different bounds (e.g., test fixtures) without forcing the constant.

## Nits
- Three nearly-identical `if (resetCount >= DEFAULT_RETRY_OPTIONS.maxFallbackCount) { debugLogger.warn(...); throw classifiedError }` blocks at `:313-318`, `:342-347`, `:378-385` cry out for an `assertFallbackBudget()` helper to enforce the same predicate everywhere ‚Äî current shape risks the next contributor adding a fourth fallback site and forgetting one of the three guards.
- The third call site at `:378-385` throws `classifiedError instanceof RetryableQuotaError ? classifiedError : error` ‚Äî slightly different from the other two sites which always throw `classifiedError`. The asymmetry is intentional (preserves quota-error type for downstream callers) but undocumented; a one-line comment naming why this site uses the conditional throw would prevent future "let's make these consistent" misrefactors.
- The constant lives in `retry.ts` as a top-level `export const`, but `RetryOptions.maxFallbackCount` reads `DEFAULT_RETRY_OPTIONS.maxFallbackCount` rather than the per-instance `maxFallbackCount` from the destructured options at `:230`-ish ‚Äî this means callers can't actually override the bound at runtime via the existing `maxAttempts`-style options pattern. Either wire the per-instance option through to the predicate, or rename the constant `MAX_FALLBACK_COUNT` (no `DEFAULT_` prefix) to make the non-overridability explicit.
- Validation-required fallback at `:342-347` shares the same global `resetCount`/`maxFallbackCount` budget as the model-fallback path. Semantically these are different operations (user-driven verification vs automatic model swap) and could merit separate budgets ‚Äî a user who legitimately verifies twice before a third attempt shouldn't be locked out of further model fallbacks. Worth raising in PR discussion.

**Verdict: merge-after-nits**
