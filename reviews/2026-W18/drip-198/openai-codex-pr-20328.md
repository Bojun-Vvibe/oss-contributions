## Rationale

Final installment of the [1/5]→[5/5] series (drip-coverage map: #20324, #20325, #20326, #20327 are the prior four). The headline change here isn't the cargo-manifest delta — that's mechanical — it's that the `verify_tui_core_boundary.py` boundary check now fires on `codex_protocol::protocol` re-exposure, not just `codex_core::` direct imports. The rule expansion is correct: the whole point of the [1-5/5] series is to force `codex-tui` to talk to the rest of the workspace through the app-server boundary, and `codex_protocol::protocol::*` is a re-export trapdoor that would silently let a TUI file reach back into the same types. The alias-tracking + literal-masking machinery is necessary; building a partial Rust use-tree parser in Python is unfortunate but reasonable given the alternatives (cargo-clippy lint, rust-analyzer driver, custom rustc plugin) are all bigger surface. Three nits: (1) the partial parser at `:60-63` and beyond doesn't handle `cfg(...)`-gated `use` statements differently from unconditional ones, so a `#[cfg(test)] use codex_protocol::protocol::X` would be flagged identically to a runtime import — that may be intentional (no test should reach into protocol either) but worth a one-line comment; (2) `use_tree_imports_root_glob` recursion has no depth bound, and a deliberately pathological 200-level-nested use-tree in a fuzzer-generated source file would stack-overflow the CI script — bound the recursion at, e.g., 32 levels and emit a diagnostic; (3) the diagnostic strings (`CODEX_PROTOCOL_MESSAGE`, `CODEX_PROTOCOL_GLOB_MESSAGE`) are good but the script doesn't emit a "did you mean" pointer to the supported alternative (the app-server protocol surface) — adding that would convert this from "you're blocked" to "you're blocked, here's the right surface" and reduce the support load on the codex-tui authors who hit the new rule.
