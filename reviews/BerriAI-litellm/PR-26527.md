## Summary

The PR's stated goal is to add a CircleCI E2E job that brings up the proxy in Docker, brings up an Anthropic-compatible CLI client image alongside it, and runs a smoke check that exercises the `/v1/messages` flow. The CI job, Dockerfile, compose file, and helper scripts under `tests/proxy_e2e_anthropic_messages_tests/claude_code/` deliver that. However, the same PR also touches 30+ unrelated production files (bedrock guardrails, the litellm content filter, MCP management endpoints, key/team/model management endpoints, RAG ingestion base, sensitive-data masker, dashboard tabs) that have nothing to do with adding a CI E2E job.

## Verdict

`request-changes`

## Reasoning

The CI job and the Anthropic-client docker scaffolding are valuable and look well-built (digest-pinned base image, workspace image caching, proper key gating). The blocker is scope: this single PR also rewrites three guardrail hooks, mutates four management endpoints, touches the sensitive-data masker, and adds dashboard team tabs. Each of those is independently reviewable production work that should not ship under a `test(ci):` title. Split the production changes out, leave the CI infra in this PR, and the test-only PR can land quickly. As-is, the diff is too large to review with the seriousness the production paths demand, and a problem in any one area would force reverting the whole CI improvement along with it.
